Free and informed consent form

This is an invitation for you to participate in the research: COGNITIVE PROCESSING OF FINANCIAL INFORMATION: What's The Rush, which has as its responsible researcher José Mauro Madeiros Velôso Soares, supervised by Prof. Dr. Cristiano Machado Costa.

This research aims to verify how financial information is processed visually. If you decide to participate, you must answer the following tasks. First, you must agree to participate in the survey, choose the language and read a snippet of financial information. The survey takes an estimated ten minutes to complete for you. To participate, you must open the task on a computer with a webcam. There will be no image recording, the webcam is used as an eye tracking device. Therefore, only a map of the gaze is created, and there is no image capture on the screen.

Throughout the research period, you can answer your questions by emailing jmauro@edu.unisinos.br . You have the right to refuse to participate or withdraw your consent, at any stage of the survey, without prejudice to you. The risks involved in this research are minimal, with only those inherent to answering questionnaires being identified, such as the participant's tiredness or annoyance. To avoid this risk, at any stage the participant can withdraw by simply closing the browser tab. The data you will provide to us will be confidential and will only be disclosed in congresses or scientific publications, with no disclosure of any data that can identify you.

These data will be kept by the researcher responsible for this research in a safe place and for a period of 5 years.


Instruction:

In this experiment, you take the role of a potential investor, considering a financial investment in the Beta Company. Your goals in carrying out the tasks are to visualize the information presented by the Beta Company and make a series of judgments about the company's performance. You will be asked about information presented by the company and what your judgment would be on the information presented.

The information you receive is not intended to represent all or all of the information necessary to make a detailed assessment of the company. Upon completing the information view, your judgment should be based solely on your decision about the information presented and tasks performed. Please read all information provided to complete the tasks.
